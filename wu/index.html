Chapter 1 (欲)

无欲，万物皆明。

Wú yù, wànwù jiē míng.

Without desire, all is clear.

而欲无欲，一也。

Ér yù wú yù, yī yě.

Yet desire and its absence are one.

Chapter 2 (自然)

万物自运，无所为也。

Wànwù zì yùn, wú suǒ wéi yě.

All things run their own course.
Nothing needs to be done.

虽为无成，因物自运。

Suī wéi wú chéng, yīn wù zì yùn.

Even though you act, nothing is accomplished.
For things run on their own.

Chapter 3 (忘)

我忘矣，如此而已。

Wǒ wàng yǐ, rúcǐ ér yǐ.

I forgot. That's it.

Chapter 4 (复)

复无。

Fù wú.

Return to nothing.

Critical Commentary

Listen to Commentary

欲 → 自然 → 忘 → 无 Desire → Naturalness → Forgetting → Nothing

The four chapters of Wu Zong Wu Pai Xue starts with the messy reality of desire. The first chapter walks through feeling it, releasing it, and ultimately realizing that wanting something and not wanting it are practically the same state. That non-dual realization brings a kind of baseline clarity, which naturally sets up everything that follows.

Once that clarity hits, intervention becomes pointless (Chapter Two). When you actually see the world exactly as it is, you realize it’s running itself just fine. Non-action isn't presented here as some rigorous ascetic discipline you have to master; it’s just the inevitable byproduct of paying attention. You stop doing things because you see that nothing needs to be done.

Then, in Chapter Three, things take a very quiet turn. The self that was doing all this observing and clarifying simply starts to fade. It just offers a simple, almost casual confession: "I forgot." It’s just what happens when there's no action left to take.

By Chapter Four, we reach the end. The number four carries traditional associations with death and finality, and the text leans all the way in with a single, blunt instruction: Return to nothing. There is no promise of the eternal Dao here. No ultimate unity, no enlightenment, no comfort. It is exactly what its title claims; the study of no school and no sect, written by a nameless author. The text doesn't try to sell you a doctrine or leave you with a warm sense of closure. It simply finishes its thought, leaves nothing behind, and goes quiet.

When desire is seen through (1), intervention becomes pointless (2). When intervention stops, the self that intervenes fades (3). When the self fades, what's left? Nothing (4).
